I am the Director of Information Technology and Assistant Dean at the College of Visual Arts and Design at the University of North Texas. I am responsible for all technology operations for the college. You can look at my LinkedIn Profile for more information on what that entails.

During my over a decade of time here, I have had a wide range of responsibilities: not all of which have been focused on information technology, strictly speaking. As such I have a high familiarity with the technology used in the arts and design fields, both software and hardware, as well as the operations of an institute of higher education and an art college.

This links to my LinkedIn profile. I keep this reasonably up to date and check it regularly. This is probably the best way to reach me other than email. My profile lists some of the initiatives and projects I have worked on for the College of Visual Arts and Design at the University of North Texas. Many of the ones you will see listed, that are not ongoing, are initiatives that my team and I launched but subsequently handed off to non-IT units: social media strategy, for example.

Github is my current preferred choice for git repositories. It is simple to use, has straightforward tutorials, and has high availability. I am using Github with Microsoft Azure, and for software development. This is what I use to host my website (this page). I have recently switched from using a MacOS dev environment, back to a Microsoft dev environment since the release of Windows Subsystem for Linux v2.

Stormwind Studios is a training resource I use to maintain my skill set and knowledge base. They provide live and on-demand bootcamp courses that are primarily geared towards passing certification tests. Their offerings cover a wide range of vendor certifications: CISCO, Juniper, VMware, AWS among others. They offer not only technical certification courses but also courses on Agile and Scrum methodologies and Project Management in general. They also offer courses on coding and general technology, such as ''Cloud Services'', ''Virualization'', and ''Networking''. This is where I took the majority of my Microsoft Azure training.

ACI Learning (previously, IT Pro TV) is another training resource I use. It has certification preparation courses, practice tests and virtual learning labs as well. The things I like most about ACI Learning are: it provices courses that are not focused solely on certification tests (for example, they offer courses on emerging technologies and simple introductory classes on a range of topics) and the class sections are broken down into < 30 minute, self-contained chunks so it is easier to proceed at your own pace.
