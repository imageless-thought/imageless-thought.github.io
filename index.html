Training and Certification

Over the years I have completed numerous training courses to maintain my skills and knowledge. Unless an official certification test is included as part of the training, I rarely take the official certification test. I find that when training is focused on passing a certification test, it is generally less useful to learners. Bootcamps, in general, are particularly bad about this: I have taken many bootcamps where the instructors only focused on material that they knew would be asked on the certification test. That is not very useful to me, as the whole purpose of the course becomes focused on gaming the testing process. Training should have a point other than to get an "officially branded" certificate.

Having said that: it is important to document your training. I recommend using a training resource that provides a certificate upon completion. Your employer may require an official certification, hopefully they reimburse you for the cost of the certification test. Otherwise, your time is better spent focusing on the material while you are in training.

I am working on creating Training Paths for my staff and will post those below as they are completed. The training paths will choose the best possible course available. For example, there are a lof of different training options for Linux and things like Git, Node.js, Powershell - the course I include on a subject will be the one that covers the material best. Below are the training courses I have completed over the past few years, broken down by OS. I also included LinkedIn Learning courses as for our purposes, they can be quite useful.

Linux Courses

The trainings and certifications for Linux are primarily based on the Linux Professional Institute (LPI) curriculum. On the course list below, the courses that follow the LPI curriculum have an LPI tag and the courses that do not have a Third-Party tag. The LPI courses are offered by LPI but also other third-party trainers such as Stormwinds Studios, ACI Learning, or Linked-In-Learning.

I am currently reviewing the courses as part of a Learning Paths project. The focus of that project is to create full learning paths, without unnecessary duplication of material, and indicate what level of training is required for various roles within an organization.

Recommended Learning Path

Essentials

Linux Essentials LPI

One of three courses that make up the LPI Essential Courses, this course should be completed by anyone working in Information Technology. It provides a reasonably good introduction to Linux including a history of Linux and Open Source Software. Besides information of the relationships between the various distributions (most are based on either Debian or Red Hat) and commonly used applications, you get the basics on supporting Linux: How to use the CLI (command line interface), Networking, and Information Security. The material in this course is not difficult to learn, but it is the foundation for all subsequent courses.

Linux Security Essentials LPI

The second of the three essential courses, this course covers preliminary information relevant in all important fields of IT security. The material in this course is also not particularly difficult if you have worked in IT for a reasonable amount, but it is the required foundation for subsequent Information Security courses.

Web Development Essentials LPI

The last of the courses in the essential series, this one is not focused on the Linux OS itself but on the web applications. Even if you are not a web developer this course should be completed by anyone who has a role beyond the helpdesk. Modern software applications are commonly developed for the Web, you should now how to install, use, and support them unless your role is purely one of ''desktop support''. Again, this course will serve as the foundation for subsequent web development copurses as well.

Core Training Modules

Linux Professional Institute LPIC-1 Linux Administrator (101-500/102-500) LPI

LPIC-1 is the first level in the professional training path for Linux. The LPIC-1 covers performing maintenance tasks on the command line, installation and configuration of a computer running Linux and configuration of basic networking. The objectives are tied to real-world job skills.

This training should be completed by anyone who will be supporting Linux desktops and/or has a role that requires building or maintaining servers. I strongly suggest that anyone who plans to work in IT as a career complete this training.

Completing this training will allow you to:

  • understand the architecture of a Linux system.
  • install and maintain a Linux workstation, including X11 and setup it up as a network client.
  • work at the Linux command line, including common GNU and Unix commands.
  • handle files and access permissions as well as system security.
  • perform easy maintenance tasks: help users, add users to a larger system, backup and restore, shutdown and reboot.
Linux Professional Institute LPIC-2 Linux Engineer (201-450/202-450) LPI

LPIC-2 is the second level in the professional training path for Linux. The LPIC-1 covers how to administer small to medium-sized mixed networks.

Completing this training will allow you to:

  • perform advanced system administration, including common tasks regarding the Linux kernel, system startup and maintenance.
  • perform advanced Management of block storage and file systems as well as advanced networking and authentication and system security, including firewall and VPN.
  • install and configure fundamental network services, including DHCP, DNS, SSH, Web servers, file servers using FTP, NFS and Samba, email delivery.
  • supervise assistants and advise management on automation and purchases.

Advanced Training Modules

The LPIC-3 courses provide information on advanced topics. These are very useful modules and if you are in a role that requires you to build or maintain servers I highly reccomend you take them, but I have not met anyone who has actually taken one of the certification exams.

Linux Professional Institute LPIC-3 Mixed Environments (300-300) LPI

The LPIC-3 Mixed Environments course covers the administration of Linux systems enterprise-wide in mixed environments.

Topics Covered:

  • Samba Basics
  • Samba and Active Directory Domains
  • Samba Share Configuration
  • Samba Client Configuration
  • Linux Identity Management and File Sharing
Linux Professional Institute LPIC-3 Security (300-303) LPI

The LPIC-3 Security course covers the administration of Linux systems enterprise-wide with an emphasis on security.

Topics Covered:

  • Cryptography
  • Host Security
  • Access Control
  • Network Security
  • Threats and Vulnerability Assessment
Linux Professional Institute LPIC-3 Virtualization and Containerization (300-305) LPI

The LPIC-3 Virtualization and Containerization course covers the administration of Linux systems enterprise-wide with an emphasis on virtualization & containerization.

Topics Covered:

  • Full Virtualization
  • Container Virtualization
  • VM Deployment and Provisioning
Linux Professional Institute LPIC-3 High Availability and Storage Clusters (300-306) LPI

The LPIC-3 High Availability and Storage Clusters course covers the administration of Linux systems enterprise-wide with an emphasis on high availability systems and storage.

Topics Covered:

  • High Availability Cluster Management
  • High Availability Cluster Storage
  • High Availability Distributed Storage
  • Single Node High Availability

Open Technology

The Open Technology courses are designed to provide the knowledge and skills of the open technology tools: DevOps tools and BSD Specialist. Both are worthwhile, though whether you need to take them or not really depends on your role.

Linux Professional Institute DevOps Tools Engineer (701) LPI

This course provides a working knowledge of DevOps-related domains such as Software Engineering and Architecture, Container and Machine Deployment, Configuration Management and Monitoring.

Topics Covered:

  • Software Engineering
  • Container Management
  • Machine Deployment
  • Configuration Management
  • Service Operations
Linux Professional Institute BSD Specialist (702) LPI

This course provides the skills in administering FreeBSD, NetBSD, and OpenBSD systems. The course covers all the three major BSD systems to ensure that you will be comfortable working in BSD-based environments of any kind.

Topics Covered:

  • BSD Installation and Software Management
  • Storage Devices and BSD Filesystems
  • Basic BSD System Administration
  • Basic BSD Network Administration
  • Basic Unix Skills
