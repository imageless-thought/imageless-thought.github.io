Training and Certification

Over the years I have completed numerous training courses to maintain my skills and knowledge. Unless an official certification test is included as part of the training, I rarely take the official certification test. I find that when training is focused on passing a certification test, it is generally less useful to learners. Bootcamps, in general, are particularly bad about this: I have taken many bootcamps where the instructors only focused on material that they knew would be asked on the certification test. That is not very useful to me, as the whole purpose of the course becomes focused on gaming the testing process. Training should have a point other than to get an "officially branded" certificate.

Having said that: it is important to document your training. I recommend using a training resource that provides a certificate upon completion. Your employer may require an official certification, hopefully they reimburse you for the cost of the certification test. Otherwise, your time is better spent focusing on the material while you are in training.

I am working on creating Training Paths for my staff and will post those below as they are completed. The training paths will choose the best possible course available. For example, there are a lof of different training options for Linux and things like Git, Node.js, Powershell - the course I include on a subject will be the one that covers the material best.
